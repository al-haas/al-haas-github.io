Welcome! I am a DPhil (PhD) Candidate in Economics at the University of Oxford (Balliol College).

Research Papers

[1] The Signalling Channel of Negative Interest Rates (May 2023 | Final version), with Oliver de Groot

Forthcoming, Journal of Monetary Economics     JME Pre-Proof    Citation    Appendix

Negative policy rates can convince markets that deposit rates will remain lower-for-longer, even when current deposit rates are constrained by zero. We analyse the optimality and effectiveness of negative rates in the context of this novel transmission channel. In a stylized model, we prove two necessary conditions for optimality: time-consistency and a preference for policy smoothing. In an estimated model, we show the signalling channel dominates banksâ€™ costly interest margin channel. However, the effectiveness of negative rates depends sensitively on the degree of policy inertia, level of reserves, and ZLB duration.
